Homotopy and Homotopy Type

Concepts
  1. Deformation Retraction
    • A deformation retraction of $X$ onto a subspace $A$ is a homotopy from $\mathbb{1}_X$ to a retraction of $X$ onto $A$.
    • A retraction of $X$ onto $A$ is a map $r:X\rightarrow X$ such that $r(X)=A$ and $r|_A=\mathbb{1}_A$.
  2. Mapping Cylinder
    • For a map $f: X\rightarrow Y$ , the mapping cylinder $M_f$ is the quotient space of the disjoint union $(X\times I)\sqcup Y$ obtained by identifying each $(x,1)\in X\times I$ with $f(x)\in Y$.
    • From a mapping cylinder, one can construct a natural deformation retraction.

Cell Complexes

Concepts
  1. Construction of a cell complex (CW complex)
    • Start with a discrete set $X^0$ (0 - cells);
    • Inductively, given maps $\varphi_\alpha:S^{n-1}\rightarrow X^{n-1}$ (attaching maps), the n - skeleton $X^n$ is the quotient space of $X^{n-1}\sqcup_\alpha D^{n}_{\alpha}$ under the identifications $x\sim \varphi_\alpha(x)$ for $x\in\partial D_\alpha^n$.
    • As a set, $X^n=X^{n-1}\sqcup_{\alpha}e^n_\alpha$ where $e_\alpha^n$ is an open n - disk (n- cell).
  2. Characteristic map
    • The map $\Phi_\alpha:D_\alpha^n\rightarrow X$ is an extension of the attaching map $\varphi_\alpha$, and is a homeomorphism from the interior of $D_\alpha^n$ onto $e^n_\alpha$.
    • The map $\Phi_\alpha$ can be taken to be the composition of maps: $D_\alpha^n\hookrightarrow X^{n-1}\sqcup_\alpha D^n_\alpha\rightarrow X^n\hookrightarrow X$ .
  3. Subcomplex
    • A subcomplex of a cell complex $X$ is a closed subspace $A\subset X$ that is a union of cells of $X$ .
    • A pair $(X,A)$ consisting of a cell complex $X$ and a subcomplex $A$ is called a CW pair.
Examples

  1. $\mathbb{C}P^n=e^0\sqcup e^2\sqcup\cdots\sqcup e^{2n}$

    • Consider $D_{+}^{2n}:={(\omega,\sqrt{1-|\omega|^2})\in\mathbb{C}^n\times\mathbb{C}:|\omega|\leq1}\cong D^{2n} $ .
  • Each vector in $S^{2n+1}$ is equivalent under the identifications $v\sim\lambda v$ to a vector in $D_{+}^{2n}$ (for the $(n+1)$-th complex coordinate of $S^{2n+1}$, saying $\omega=r\mathrm{e}^{i\theta}$, divide it by $\mathrm{e}^{i\theta}$ to make it a nonnegative real number).
    • The latter vector in $D^{2n}+$ is unique if its last coordinate is nonzero. If the last coordinate is zero, we have just the identifications $v\sim\lambda v$ for $v\in \partial D^{2n}+\cong S^{2n-1}$ .
  • In short, we get a map: $D_{+}^{2n}\rightarrow \mathbb{C}P^n$, and we have $\mathbb{C}P^n=\mathrm{int}(D_{+}^{2n})\sqcup(\partial D^{2n}_+/\sim)=e^{2n}\sqcup\mathbb{C}P^{n-1}$.

Oprations on Spaces

Concepts

  1. Products

  2. Quotients

  3. Suspension

    • The suspension $SX$ is the quotient of $X\times I$ by collapsing $X\times{0}$ to one poing and $X\times{1}$ to another point.

    • The cone $CX$ is the quotient space $(X\times I)/(X\times{0})$. So one can also regard $SX$ as the union of two copies of the cone $CX$.

  4. Join

    • The join of $X$ and $Y$, denoted by $X*Y$ , is the quotient space of $X\times Y\times I$ by the identification $(x,y_1,0)\sim(x,y_2,0)$ and $(x_1,y,1)\sim (x_2,y,1)$.
    • $X*Y$ is the space of all line segments joining points in $X$ to points in $Y$.
    • $X*Y$ is formal linear combinations $t_1x+t_2y$ with $0\leq t_i\leq 1$ and $t_1+t_2=1$ , subject to the rules $0x+1y=y$ and $1x+0y=x$ (convex combination of $X$ and $Y$).

  1. Wedge sum

    • “One point union”，denoted by $X\vee Y$.

  2. Smash product

    • $X\wedge Y:=(X\times Y)/(X\vee Y)$.

    • It’s a reduced version of $X\times Y$ obtained by collapsing away the parts that are not genuinely a product, the separate factors $X$ and $Y$. (Recall the distinction between direct product and tensor product.)

    • It’s the “tensor product” of pointed spaces, the identifying procedure is meant to create a new base point. (The adjunction property of it is to be learned…)
